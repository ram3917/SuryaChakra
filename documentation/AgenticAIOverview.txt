1. The questions your agents must answer
For refurbished ICE → EV autos (no swapping, DC charging focus), your system should answer:

How many autos can realistically become EVs (by age, permits, policy caps)?

How do these autos operate daily (km/day, working hours, routes)?

Where are public DC chargers located in Hyderabad (especially within ORR), and how dense is coverage in auto-heavy zones?

If an auto switches from petrol/CNG to EV, how much CO₂ is avoided and what is the running-cost saving per km/month?

Each agent is “responsible” for one of these dimensions.

2. Agent 1 – Auto stock & retrofit potential agent
Goal: Build a clean dataset of how many autos exist and how many can/are being converted.

Key inputs and sources

State plans to retrofit around 1.2–1.26 lakh ICE autos with electric kits, with up to 100% subsidy for drivers under a “Telangana Auto EV Subsidy” scheme.

Within Hyderabad ORR limits, GO 263 permits 40,000 new eco‑friendly autos:

20,000 electric

10,000 LPG

10,000 CNG

Plus 25,000 retrofitted petrol/diesel autos to eco‑friendly variants.

Earlier subsidy guidelines targeted old autos (10+ years) with ₹15,000 per vehicle against a retrofit cost of ₹75,000–₹90,000.

What the agent does

Scrapes / queries:

Telangana Transport / CFST / RTAs for auto-rickshaw registrations and permits in Hyderabad + ORR.

Policy docs and GO summaries that specify caps, age limits, and retrofit eligibility.

Normalizes into a autos_hyd table with columns like:

year_of_registration, fuel_type, age_band, location_zone (inside ORR / outside), is_retrofittable, permit_type.

Questions it answers

“How many 10+ year old petrol/diesel autos are there within ORR that can be retrofitted?”

“Given current schemes, what is the max addressable market for refurbished EV autos?”

3. Agent 2 – Usage pattern & duty-cycle agent
Goal: Capture how refurbished EV autos actually work day-to-day.

Key ideas and sources

Studies and guidebooks on Indian auto-rickshaws show typical urban e‑auto duty cycles: 80–140 km per day, 8–12 hours on road, with a mix of short trips, waiting time, and a few longer hauls.

Local pilots (e.g., e‑autos at Kacheguda railway station) demonstrate common station-based use cases and shift timings.

What the agent does

Collects from:

Literature and case studies (WRI e‑auto guidebook, Hyderabad electric mobility study) for baseline km/day and load factors.

If you add your own survey later (Google Forms or in-person), this agent ingests CSVs of real driver data (km/day, trips/day, breaks, preferred stands).

Writes into an auto_usage_profiles table with fields such as:

segment (refurbished_EV_auto), km_per_day, hours_per_day, days_per_month, energy_use_kwh_per_km, typical_routes (as simple IDs or corridor labels).

Questions it answers

“Do refurbished EV autos need 1 or 2 DC charge sessions per day without SuryaChakra?”

“What fraction of their shift is realistically under sun and available for solar top-up?”

4. Agent 3 – Public DC charging locator agent
Goal: Map where public DC chargers are, so you can match infrastructure to auto demand.

Key inputs and sources

TGREDCO / GHMC lists of sanctioned and installed EV chargers: many are on government land, ORR entry/exit points, bus stops, parks, substations, etc.

City news lists show specific charger locations (e.g., Nagole Track DC Charger, Mother Teresa Statue DC Charger, Saroornagar Stadium DC Charger).

Aggregator maps (e‑AMRIT, CPO / directory sites) provide more DC fast-charger points and coordinates.

What the agent does

Scrapes these lists / APIs and geocodes addresses to lat/long.

Filters for DC chargers only (Bharat DC‑001, CCS, CHAdeMO, etc., and power ≥ 15 kW).

Writes into dc_chargers_hyd with:

lat, lon, power_kw, connector_type, operator, location_type (park, RTO, petrol pump, mall, roadside), zone (inside ORR / outside).

Questions it answers

“Where are DC chargers relative to dense auto operating zones?”

“Which auto corridors are poorly served by DC charging?”

5. Agent 4 – CO₂ and cost-comparison agent
Goal: Quantify CO₂ avoided and cost savings when an old ICE auto becomes a refurbished EV auto charged on DC.

Key inputs and assumptions

Retrofit kits are typically 4–8 kWh, with total conversion costs ₹1.2–2 lakh per auto; state is planning up to 100% subsidy.

FAME / national documents give standard emission factors and guidance for EV charging vs petrol/CNG (kg CO₂ per litre of fuel vs per kWh grid electricity).

What the agent does

Maintains reference tables:

emission_factors – e.g., kg CO₂/litre petrol, kg CO₂/kg CNG, kg CO₂/kWh (Telangana grid).

tariffs – local fuel prices and EV charging tariffs (Rs/litre, Rs/kg, Rs/kWh, DC fast-charging rates).

Joins with auto_usage_profiles to compute:

Daily and monthly fuel use and cost for ICE auto.

Daily and monthly kWh and charging cost for refurbished EV auto.

CO₂ avoided = baseline ICE emissions − EV charging emissions.

Questions it answers

“For a typical refurbished EV auto (100 km/day), how many kg of CO₂ are avoided per year vs petrol?”

“What is the per‑km and per‑month cost saving in rupees?”

This is where you can plug in SuryaChakra later: adding solar energy reduces kWh taken from grid DC chargers, further lowering CO₂ and cost.

6. Orchestration overview – how the agents work together
A simple, robust workflow for now:

Nightly data refresh

Auto stock agent runs once a week (policy and registration numbers don’t change daily).

DC charger agent runs nightly to catch newly listed chargers.

CO₂ / price agent updates fuel and electricity prices daily or weekly.

Weekly analysis job

Reads from autos_hyd, auto_usage_profiles, dc_chargers_hyd, emission_factors, and tariffs.

Produces:

Zone‑wise “EV‑auto potential vs DC charger density” table.

Per‑auto and fleet‑level CO₂ and cost comparison results.

Exports these as CSVs and some standard plots/maps for your SuryaChakra deck.

Map creation

A geospatial script (or notebook) uses autos_hyd + dc_chargers_hyd to generate:

Heatmaps of auto density and retrofittable ICE autos.

DC charger locations and coverage rings (e.g., 1 km radius) overlaid.
